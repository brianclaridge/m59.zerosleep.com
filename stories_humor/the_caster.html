On a calm Meridian evening, on a walk back from the island
I met an ancient caster, I could hear his leg bones creak
He didn't bat an eye at cave orcs or the wailing in darkness
At the Spring we drank our health back, the mage began to speak

He said, son I've made a life out of choosing the right mushrooms 
And knowing who my foes are, and the threats they pose to me 
Your face tells me your story, you don't yet know ways of magic 
I can teach you of the secrets, for just two of your inkies.

So I handed him the mushies, and he ate them in an instant,
Even water-satisfied, he weren't too full to eat
And then in the sloshing darkness, with orc cries in the distance 
He said these sagely words to me, his eyes now burning heat.
