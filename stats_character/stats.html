Character Creation: Stats
Might: Influences the amount of damage you inflict per stroke for physical attacks.

Intellect: Impacts learning of spells and skills. Players with high Intellect can learn and advance more quickly.

Stamina: Players with high Stamina will be less affected by reduced Vigor than those with lower Stamina and will regain Health Points faster.

Agility: Improves "Dodge" ability and capacity for learning new skills.

Mysticism: Crucial for mana replenishment, as well as spell learning. A high mysticism score will allow you to learn spells faster.

Aim: Impacts your chances of hitting with almost any attack. Critical for fighter/mage combination characters.

Pure Mage by Alfred_Montbanks Training Manual
Stat Amount Info
Might 30 Though you may think this isn't important for a mage, try lugging around all those heavy spell regents with a weak character. Make this stat 30, with SuperStrength (Kraanen spell) this will be boosted up to 50.
Intellect 50 The higher your intellect, the more schools of magic you can aquire and the faster you will advance in spell levels. Since the ultimate goal of the pure mage is the mastery of as much magic as possible, make this one 50.
Stamina 24 - 29 Stamina is much more important since the Revelation's update. This affects how quickly you regain both your Hit Points (health, how much life you have left) and your mana (spell power). This also determines your maximum hit points. You should have 29 points left over to assign to Stamina. You may wish to set your aim at 45 and leave 24 points for stamina. It's up to you. Your maximum hit points is 100 + your stamina but it is hard to go over 120 no matter what.
Agility 1 Ugh. This is the evil Weaponcraft statistic. Useless for the pure mage. Make this 1.
Mysticism 50 This is THE skill for a mage. Critical for getting the Mana (spell power) needed to cast your spells (see Mana Table for details). Also affects how powerfull your spells are when they are cast. Make this one 50 also. The common belief is the mage with mysticism below 50 is a fool.
Aim 40 There is a little flexibility here. Many will tell you to set your aim as close to 50 as possible since it affects your touch spells. I like 40 for Aim. I find that you hit quite often (spell % will also affect how often you hit so if you get your touch spells to 99% then 40 should be good enough for aim) and you can put the extra stat points towards.
